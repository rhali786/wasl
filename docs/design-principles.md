# Wird — Design Principles

**A Qur'an review companion.** Working title and product name: **Wird**
("a daily devotional return").

> **Platform:** iPhone, mobile-first. **For:** intermediate Qur'an
> learners who already read Arabic and have memorized surahs. **Posture:**
> review, not content.

---

## 1. Thesis

> *"Show me where I am right now, and what deserves my next five minutes."*

The user already reads Arabic, has memorized surahs, and recognizes many
words. They don't need more content — they need a calm system to review
what they already carry, and one clear answer to "what now?" in any spare
five minutes.

**What it is:** a calm, current review companion. Repeated short sessions.
Honest word-familiarity tracking driven by reading behavior, not
self-grading.

**What it is not:** a beginner Arabic course, a tafsir app, a full mushaf,
a gamified language platform, or a flashcard deck.

The metric serves the Qur'an, not the other way around. Every decision
below is downstream of that.

---

## 2. Principles — The Three Laws + the working philosophy

These override every other decision:

1. **The Qur'an is the hero.** Nothing competes with the Arabic text. UI
   recedes. The text advances.
2. **Returning is an exhale.** Every re-open should feel like *sakinah*,
   not a performance review. No guilt, no debt, no streak alarm.
3. **Truth over comfort.** The tree grows only when words are genuinely
   known. Beauty serves honesty.

Working principles that follow from the Three Laws:

- **Review over exposure.** The home answers one question and starts in a
  single tap.
- **Return over streaks.** Coming back is celebrated; the missed hour is
  never punished.
- **Honesty by construction.** Metrics move from real behavior, not from
  buttons pressed for show.
- **Intrinsic, not extrinsic.** The reward is the thing itself becoming
  clearer — never points bolted on top.

---

## 3. What this is not / out of scope

| Not this | Because |
|---|---|
| XP, levels, laurel wreaths | Vanity. Disconnected from actual comprehension. |
| Guilt-streak flames | Creates return-as-debt. Opposite of *sakinah*. A streak's only power is the threat of losing it — the moment it appears, the center of gravity shifts from *return* to *don't break the chain*. |
| Cartoon mascots | Childish register. Wrong for Qur'an. |
| Progress bars racing to 100% | Suggests completion. Qur'an review has no ceiling. |
| Dense home screen with many doors | Forces decisions. Don't make the user think. |
| Loud animations / confetti / celebration particles | Breaks the quiet. |
| Teaching letters or beginner grammar | Out of scope — script is not the friction point for this user. |
| Root / morphology as a core feature | Out of scope for MVP. |
| A full tafsir or full mushaf replacement | Out of scope. |
| AI tutoring or chat | Narrow, honest AI only (build-time glosses, adaptive hints) — never a chat tutor. |
| Social feeds, friends, leaderboards | Out of scope. |
| Word audio | Out of scope for MVP. |
| "I Knew It" confirmation button | Removed. Status changes via reading behavior only. |
| Two contrasting visual environments | One unified garden-like environment, shifts with time of day. |

The Living Tree is the **one** permitted gamification element —
because it is honest, never dies, and is grounded in Qur'an (Ibrāhīm
14:24: *"A good word is like a good tree — its roots firm, its branches
in the sky."*).
