01 — Thesis

A training log for the Qur'an, not another place to read it.

The user already reads Arabic, has memorized surahs, and recognizes many words. They don't need more content. They need a beautiful system to review what they're already carrying — and to know, in any spare five minutes, exactly where to put their attention.

What it is

A calm, current review companion. Repeated short sessions. Honest word-familiarity tracking. One clear answer to "what now?"

What it is not

Not a beginner Arabic course, not a tafsir app, not a full mushaf, not a gamified language platform, not a flashcard deck.

Opinion: the original PRD's strongest line is the one to build the whole product around — the metric serves the Qur'an, not the other way around. Every decision below is downstream of that.

02 — User & Problem

The problem isn't access. It's direction.

Existing tools optimize for reading, for teaching beginners, or for exposing learners to volume. None answers the intermediate reviewer's real question: of everything I already half-know, what deserves attention now?

Reads Arabic

Script is not a barrier. Recognition of letters is not the friction point.

Has ḥifẓ

Memorized surahs exist and matter most — yet most tools ignore them.

Wants honesty

Tired of inflated streaks and vanity counts. Wants progress that's true.

03 — Philosophy

Calm, current, and quietly honest.

  • Review over exposure. The home answers one question and starts in a single tap.
  • Return over streaks. Coming back is celebrated; the missed hour is never punished.
  • Honesty by construction. Metrics move from real behaviour, not from buttons pressed for show.
  • Intrinsic, not extrinsic. The reward is the thing itself becoming clearer — never points bolted on top.
  • Sakinah on return. Opening the app should feel like settling, not like clocking in.
04 — Decisions Locked

What we settled.

The choices made through this design process, with the reasoning that drove each.

Platform
iPhone, mobile-first. Five-minute sessions between meetings and after ṣalāh are phone behaviour. The app decides everything it can so the user never has to think.
Architecture
Local-first. No backend for v1. The Qur'an is a fixed corpus of ~77,400 words / 6,236 ayāt — a few MB of JSON that never changes, for one user. No server, no auth, no API.
LingQ import
A one-time migration script, not a product feature. You import your vocabulary seed once; it should never become an in-app pipeline. (Verify the API version — v3 vs v2 — and capture extended_status so "known" words aren't collapsed.)
Qur'an text
Tanzil — the verified standard. Uthmani for display, simple/imlaai available for matching.
Word meanings
A word-by-word gloss dataset, not the Quranic Arabic Corpus. The Corpus is morphology-focused, frozen, and GPL-licensed — avoid it. Roots are out of MVP scope anyway.
Word identity
Store both forms. Diacritized for display; a normalized key (tashkīl stripped, alif/hamza/yā' unified) for matching and status.
Meanings
Hidden by default. Revealing a meaning is itself the signal that you didn't know it.
Session
Whenever the app is in focus. Background → session ends. Return → new session.
Onboarding
Three answers. Pick your memorized surahs → pick what you're currently memorizing → Long Hike is the 10 longest surahs by a predefined list. Done.
Visual system
Two rooms. A calm green garden (home) and a warm dark study (reader), joined by a single gold thread of light.

How this evolved from the original PRD

  • The PRD's "MVP" was really a v1.0. Cut to a true MVP — the reader loop on one screen.
  • Full REST API + multi-user schema → dropped for local-first.
  • Manual promote/demote statuses → an automatic engine (reading promotes, tapping demotes) with one optional override.
  • Added two rules the PRD didn't have: the Known ceiling and memorized-surah protection.
  • "Avoid vanity metrics" → made structural: returns replace streaks; no XP exists to inflate.
  • Four reader modes → Review (reflow) ships first; exact Mushaf-page layout is the fast-follow.
  • "Beautiful, calm, not gamified" (unspecified) → a defined design language: two rooms, the goodly-tree, the fog metaphor.
  • "No AI tutoring" → narrow, honest AI: build-time contextual glosses and adaptive weak-word hints.
05 — The Core Loop

Reading is measurement.

The single key activity is the tap. With meanings hidden, the act of tapping a word is the user honestly admitting "I don't know this cold." That gesture is how the app learns what you know — every metric flows from it.

Engine A — Silent promotion

Read a word without tapping = a clean read. Clean reads accumulate and quietly promote the word. Reading is the +1.

Engine B — Tap demotion

Tap a word to reveal its meaning = you needed to check. The word quietly steps down a level. Tapping is the −1.

The override: the reveal carries one optional button — "I knew it" — for the curiosity tap. It cancels the demotion. It is the only manual action in the whole status system. Closing without it = the default step down.

The status ladder

Default on import
1

2

3

Passed consistently — the ceiling for reading alone
4

The ceiling rule & the memorizer trap. A ḥāfiẓ can recite Al-Ikhlas flawlessly while not knowing what يُولَد means. So reading alone tops out at Recognized; reaching Known always requires one real meaning-check. A fully-memorized surah correctly opens near 0% Known — that's the honest baseline, and where the most progress will come from.

The reveal panel — disciplined

Shows

The contextual meaning for this occurrence, and the optional "I knew it." Nothing else.

Never shows

Root, lemma, grammar labels, status numbers, dictionary-source pickers, share/bookmark, "click for more."

06 — Metrics & Honesty

Returns, not streaks.

Because status moves from behaviour and not button-presses, the numbers can't be gamed. Promoting 30 words in 60 seconds is structurally impossible. Integrity is built in, not policed.

Did I return?

Soft dots for today's sessions. Four is celebrated; zero is not shamed.

Did words move?

Honest promotions and demotions — real threshold crossings.

What's clearer?

Known-word growth by surah over 7 days, 30 days, 12 weeks.

Opinion: a streak is anti-sakinah by design — its only power is the threat of losing it. The moment a streak counter lands on the home screen, the centre of gravity shifts from return to don't break the chain, and the app becomes the thing the user left LingQ to escape. So there is no streak, and no XP.

Motion language

  • One breath, ~5–6 seconds. The tree sways and the lamp breathes on the same calm rhythm — slow enough to breathe along with.
  • The clearing. Word tints transition softly (~400ms) as status changes; the page brightens over a session.
  • Restraint. One signature motion per room. Reduced-motion is always respected.
08 — The Reader

The page comes first. Tapping is the quiet thing you do on top.

It reads like a real mushaf so that ḥifẓ page-memory stays intact — you remember where an ayah sits. One ayah is gently anchored (brighter, larger); the rest of the page stays as dimmed context. Tap any word, any time, to reveal its meaning.

Review mode — ships first

Comfortable reflow at phone-friendly size. The full tap → reveal → status engine lives here. The active line scales up for thumb-friendly targets.

Mushaf page — fast-follow

Exact Madani layout for page-memory, using page-specific glyph fonts. Harder to map words to meanings, so it comes after the core loop works.

The reveal bar pins to the bottom (thumb zone) and never covers the ayah. Dismiss by tapping the page or swiping down. Local data means zero latency; the panel springs up. Word audio auto-plays on reveal (post-MVP), tying recognition to recitation.

09 — The Home

Return to sakinah.

A calm garden with a single living tree, a time-aware greeting, and one quiet invitation. No dashboard, no tab-bar of games, no level badge. Just a calm room with something alive in it.

  • The greeting shifts with the time of day — "the morning is open," "the night is still" — meeting you in the day's rhythm.
  • One invitation: the single best next review, phrased as welcome — "Az-Zalzalah is near · nine words rest close to known" — with one word: Return.
  • Returns today as soft dots that light up. Never a streak.
  • The tree is rooted in your solid words and grows its canopy with your Known words. It breathes. It never dies.

12 — Build Order

Smallest honest thing first.

  • Sprint 0 — Data. Load Tanzil corpus + word-by-word glosses into local JSON. Normalize forms. Run the build-time AI pass for contextual glosses. Import the LingQ seed once.
  • Sprint 1 — Review reader. Reflow mushaf-style page, anchored ayah, tint-by-status, tap → reveal → automatic status + "I knew it." This alone is a usable product for a week.
  • Sprint 2 — Garden home. Breathing tree, time-aware greeting, the one invitation, returns dots.
  • Sprint 3 — Analytics. Known-word growth, returns, opportunity surahs, 12-week view.
  • Sprint 4 — Fast-follows. Exact Mushaf-page mode, word audio, weak-word mode, adaptive hints.

Opinion: the biggest risk is the PRD's own Risk #1 — building replaces studying. Ship Sprint 1, use it for a real week, and let what you actually miss dictate Sprint 2. Resist the urge to build all five before opening it once.
